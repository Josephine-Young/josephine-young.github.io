Vector Semantics and Embeddings

2022-11-01 8 min read # NLP # Reading Note # Speech and Language Processing

The underlying assumptions and hypothesis about embeddings, and the fruits of out vector-embedding transition——Word2Vec.

Speech and Language Processing - Chapter 6 Note

Some linguistic principles and assumptions:

  • Distributional Hypothesis:a linguistic assumption about the link between similarity in how words are distributed and similarity in their meanings.
  • Principle of Contrast: A difference in linguistic form is always associated with a difference in meaning. Practically speaking, two synonyms with the same propositional meaning are still not identical.

“It will be useful to shift from talking about relations between word senses (like synonymy) to relations between words (like similarity).”

  • word association -> semantic field and topic models, semantic frames
  • Note that word association and word similarity are both important concepts but have distinguised meanings.
  • In linguistics, sentiment could be described and measured from three dimensions, namely valence, arousal and dominance.

Based on the above intuition, principles and related linguistic research, the concept of "embeddings" emerge.
Embeddings: Derive a word's meaning in a multidimensional semantic space from the distributions of neighboring words.
Sometimes embeddings are sparse like in TF-IDF models, but mostly dense.

1.Word Vector

1.1 TF-IDF

  1. Intuition
    There is a paradox about word frequency: Frequent word occurrences are more important, but words that are too frequent or even ubiquitous, are unimportant and non-discriminative, like the or good. There are two common solutions to this paradox:TF-IDF weighting and PPMI algorithm.

  2. Definition
    Term Frequence (TF),frequency of word www in documnt ddd:

    tft,d=log⁡10(count(w,d)+1) \mathrm{tf}_{t,d}=\log_{10}(\mathrm{count}(w,d)+1) tft,d​=log10​(count(w,d)+1)

    Inverse of Document Frequency (IDF), giving a higher weight to words that only occur in a few documents.

    idft,d=log⁡10(Ndft)\mathrm{idf}_{t,d}=\log_{10}\big(\frac{N}{\mathrm{df}_t}\big) idft,d​=log10​(dft​N​)

    Thus, TF-IDF weighted value for a word ttt in document ddd is:

    wt,d=tft,d×idftw_{t,d}=\mathrm{tf}_{t,d}\times\mathrm{idf}_{t} wt,d​=tft,d​×idft​

    In this way, ubiquitous words and important/discriminative words with substantial meanings could be distinguised.

1.2 Positive Pointwise Mutual Information (PPMI)

  1. Pointwise Mutual Information (PMI)
    mutual information between two random variables X,YX,YX,Y:

I(X,Y)=∑x∑yP(x,y)log⁡2P(x,y)P(x)P(y) I(X,Y)=\sum_x\sum_yP(x,y)\log_2\frac{P(x,y)}{P(x)P(y)} I(X,Y)=x∑​y∑​P(x,y)log2​P(x)P(y)P(x,y)​

Deriving from this equation, PMI is defined as:

PMI(w,c)=log⁡2P(w,c)P(w)P(c) PMI(w,c)=\log_2\frac{P(w,c)}{P(w)P(c)} PMI(w,c)=log2​P(w)P(c)P(w,c)​

The intuition is to measure how more often the target word www and the context word ccc co-occur than the expected priori (if they were independent of each other).

  1. PPMI

    Negative PMI values tend to be reliable unless the corpus are enormous, and it’s not clear about what “unrelatedness” means, therefore only positive PMI values are included:

    PPMI(w,c)=max⁡(log⁡2P(w,c)P(w)P(c),0)\mathrm{PPMI}(w,c)=\max\big(\log_2\frac{P(w,c)}{P(w)P(c)},0\big) PPMI(w,c)=max(log2​P(w)P(c)P(w,c)​,0)

    To sum, TF-IDF is measure from term-document dimension, and PPMI is derived from term-term dimension, showing the association between target and context words.

2. Embeddings——Word2vec Algorithms

static embeddings (word2vec, GloVe) ——> contextual embeddings(BERT)

Some intuitions:

  • Why short,dense word vectors usually perform better than long,sparse ones?
    Shorter vectors constitute a smaller vector space with less dimensions than longer vectors, thus the distance between similar words and two random words will be closer in a meaningful way; Also, representing words with less dimensions enables the model to learn less weights, helping with generalization and the avoidance of overfitting.
  • Intuitions behind SGNS&word2vec: Use the corpus as implicitly supervised training data to train a binary classifier to predict "Is word www likely to occur near word ccc"? Then use the weights as word embeddings while discarding the task output. That means the learning process isn't completely seperated from the prediction task but a part of it ——>self-supervison.

It turns out that dense embeddings like word2vec actually have an elegant mathematical relationship with sparse embeddings like PPMI, in which word2vec can be seen as implicitly optimizing a shifted version of a PPMI matrix.[Levy and Goldberg,2014c]

2.1 Skip-Gram with Negative Samples (SGNS)

All embedding algorithms suffer from inherent variability, due to random initialization and random negetive sampling, and individual documents in a collection may strongly impact the resulting embeddings.

  • Train multiple embeddings with bootstrap sampling over documents and average the results when embeddings are used to study word associations in particular corpora.
