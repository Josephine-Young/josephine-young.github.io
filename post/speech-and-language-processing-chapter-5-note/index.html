Clearly, normalization and standardization won't change the distribution of input data. The difference mainly is the range: zero mean or between [0,1]. The question is more about which to use.

Regularization

Interpretation of L1 and L2

Geometrically, L1 is the Manhatten distance between current weight values θ^\hat\thetaθ^ and the starting point θ0\theta_0θ0​, while L2 means Euclidean distance.

Empirically, L2 prefers weight vectors with many small weights, while L1 prefers sparse solutions with some larger weights but many more weights set to zero. Thus L1 regularisation leads to much sparser weight vectors, i.e. fewer features.

Regularization's Bayesian Interpretation as constraints on the prior of how weights shold look:
Different regularizations implicitly represent different assumptions of the weight's distribution.
